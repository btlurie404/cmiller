Lupus-Related Internet Sites

  • Lupus Foundation of America
    Includes collection of Commonly Asked Questions, up-to-date information on lupus, health forums of ongoing supportive talk among people coping with lupus, list of Chapters, calendar of events of local lupus chapters around the country, and Research & Resource Library.

  • American Academy of Dermatology
    Public information section is under construction, but their "Find a Physician" section will help locate member dermatologists in your city and state.

  • American Autoimmune Related Diseases Association, Inc.
    Includes patient information on more than fifty autoimmune related diseases, research updates, suggestions on coping with an autoimmune disease, press releases, and link to online system for communicating with your member of Congress.

  • American College of Rheumatology
    Includes a directory of rheumatologists, frequently asked rheumatology questions, comprehensive list of links to rheumatology resources on the Internet, and more.

  • Arthritis Foundation

  • Fibromyalgia Sites

  • Healthline Lupus Erythematosus Learning Center

  • Lupus Canada (in English and French)

  • Lupus Multiplex Registry and Repository - contains information on systemic lupus erythematosus. Its purpose is to inform as well as recruit families for the repository. Sponsored by the National Institute of Health and the Oklahoma Medical Research Foundation.

  • MUMS National Parent-to-Parent Network
    National Parent-to-Parent organization for parents or care providers of a child with any disability, rare or not so rare disorder, chromosomal abnormality or health condition.

  • National Institute of Arthritis, Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases (NIAMS)
    Includes clinical studies, health information, scientific resources, news and events.

  • National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Disease (NIDDK)
    Patient information on kidney diseases and treatment options.

  • National Stroke Association

  • Road Back Foundation
    Important information for patients with Rheumatoid Arthritis, Scleroderma, Lupus, Polymyositis, Reiter's Syndrome, Psoriatic Arthritis, Ankylosing Spondylitis, Fibromyalgia or Juvenile Rheumatoid Arthritis. Rather than help you "cope" or "learn to live with your disease" they offer a safe, effective treatment that will put you on the road back to a productive life.

  • Scleroderma Foundation

  • Sjogrens Syndrome Foundation, Inc.
